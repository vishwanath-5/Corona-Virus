Covid 19 - Stay Home Stay Safe

Coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19)

Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is an infectious disease caused by SARS-CoV-2. It was first identified in Wuhan, China in 2019 and later declared a global pandemic by the World Health Organization (WHO) in March 2020. The disease affected millions of lives worldwide and had major impacts on health, economy, and daily life.

How to Protect Yourself

Do’s

  • Cover your nose and mouth while sneezing or coughing.
  • Wash hands frequently with soap or sanitizer.
  • Consult a doctor if you feel unwell.
  • Sneeze into your elbow, not hands.
  • Avoid crowded places.
  • Maintain social distance.
  • Take proper rest and sleep.
  • Drink water and eat healthy food.

Don’ts

  • Do not touch your face unnecessarily.
  • Avoid shaking hands.
  • Do not spit in public.
  • Do not self-medicate.
  • Do not litter used tissues.

FAQ's - Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is SARS-CoV-2?

SARS-CoV-2 is a novel coronavirus first identified in 2019, responsible for COVID-19.

2. What is COVID-19?

COVID-19 is a respiratory illness caused by SARS-CoV-2.

3. Where did COVID-19 start?

It was first reported in Wuhan, China.

4. What are the symptoms?

Common symptoms include fever, cough, fatigue, and loss of taste or smell.
